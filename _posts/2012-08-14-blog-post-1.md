# In this blog post, I share my EEG/ERP analysis pipeline for extracting the N2 component.

 The example dataset used here comes from one of my previously published studies.

- [Download the dataset](#)
- [Download all analysis scripts](#)
- [Read the published article](#)

## Study Overview

In this study, we investigated whether inhibitory control is involved in voluntary language switching by applying transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) over the right dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (rDLPFC), a brain region associated with inhibitory control.

The experiment included three within-subject factors:

- **tDCS Session**: A-tDCS, C-tDCS, S-tDCS  
- **Language**: L1, L2  
- **Language Sequence**: repeat, switch  

The goal of this pipeline is to extract and analyze the N2 ERP component from the EEG recordings.

---

# ERP Processing Pipeline

The complete ERP pipeline consists of six main steps.

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 1. Load EEG Recordings and Merge Datasets</strong></summary>

<p>
The original EEG recordings were collected using a 64-channel EEG system from eegmagine (ANT Neuro) and stored in BrainVision <code>.vhdr</code> format.
</p>

<p>
Each participant completed multiple recording blocks during the experiment.
</p>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to import the raw EEG recordings into EEGLAB, merge all recording blocks into a single continuous dataset, and prepare the data for preprocessing and ERP analysis.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Initialize EEGLAB Environment</h2>

<p>
The script first initializes the EEGLAB environment and loads the BrainVision import plugin (<code>BVA-io</code>).
</p>

<pre><code>
clc;
clear

eeglab_root = 'D:\setup\matlab\toolbox\eeglab11_0_5_4b';
addpath(genpath(eeglab_root));

plugin_askinstall('BVA-io', [], 1);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Define Input and Output Directories</h2>

<p>
The input and output directories are defined for loading raw EEG recordings and saving merged datasets.
</p>

<pre><code>
root_dir='D:\0EEG\0_raw_data';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\1_append';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Loop Through Experimental Conditions</h2>

<p>
The script loops through the three stimulation conditions:
A-tDCS, C-tDCS, and S-tDCS.
</p>

<pre><code>
stimulation={'A-tDCS' 'C-tDCS' 'S-tDCS'};
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load EEG Recordings</h2>

<p>
For each participant, all BrainVision EEG recordings (<code>.vhdr</code>) are identified and loaded into EEGLAB.
</p>

<pre><code>
data_list=dir('*.vhdr');

EEG = pop_loadbv(perdon_out_dir,data_list(k).name);

EEG = eeg_checkset( EEG );
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Merge Recording Blocks</h2>

<p>
The individual recording blocks are merged into one continuous EEG dataset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_mergeset( ALLEEG, [1 2 3 4 5 6], 0);
</code></pre>

<p>
Merging datasets simplifies preprocessing and creates one participant-level dataset for ERP analysis.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Save Merged Dataset</h2>

<p>
The merged dataset is saved as an EEGLAB <code>.set</code> file for later preprocessing and ERP analysis.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_saveset(EEG,[person_list(i).name,'.set'],Sti_out_dir);
</code></pre>




<hr>

<p>
At the end of this step:
</p>

<ul>
<li>all EEG recordings have been imported into EEGLAB</li>
<li>multiple recording blocks have been merged</li>
<li>participant-level <code>.set</code> files have been generated</li>
<li>the datasets are ready for preprocessing and ICA analysis</li>
</ul>

</p>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 2. EEG Preprocessing Before ICA</strong></summary>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to preprocess the merged EEG datasets before running Independent Component Analysis (ICA).
</p>

<p>
Several preprocessing procedures are applied to improve data quality, including channel locations, resampling, high pass(0.1) and low pass(30) filtering, re-referencing, exclude bad channels and epoch. 
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Initialize EEGLAB Environment</h2>

<p>
The script first initializes the EEGLAB environment and defines the input and output directories.
</p>

<pre><code>
clc;
clear;

eeglab_root = 'D:\setup\matlab\toolbox\eeglab11_0_5_4b';
addpath(genpath(eeglab_root));

root_dir='D:\0EEG\1_append';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\2_before_ICA';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load EEG Datasets</h2>

<p>
The script loops through all stimulation conditions and loads each participant’s merged EEGLAB <code>.set</code> dataset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_loadset(data_list(j).name);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Remove Additional Channels</h2>

<p>
Additional bipolar channels (<code>BIP</code>) that are not required for ERP analysis are removed from the dataset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_select( EEG,'nochannel',{...});
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Channel Location Editing</h2>

<p>
Standard electrode locations are added using the standard BESA electrode template. The CPz channel label is also corrected.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG=pop_chanedit(EEG, 'lookup', ... );
</code></pre>

<hr>


<h2>Resampling</h2>

<p>
The EEG data are resampled to 500 Hz to reduce file size and computational load while preserving ERP signals.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_resample( EEG, 500);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Filtering</h2>

<p>
A high-pass filter at 0.1 Hz and a low-pass filter at 30 Hz are applied to remove slow drifts and high-frequency noise.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_eegfiltnew(EEG, ...
    'locutoff', 0.1, ...
    'hicutoff',30);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Re-referencing</h2>

<p>
The EEG signals are re-referenced to the average of the left and right mastoid electrodes (M1 and M2).
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_reref( EEG, [13 19] );
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Bad Channel Detection</h2>

<p>
Bad channels are automatically identified using variance-based z-scores. Channels with abnormal variance are temporarily excluded before ICA.
</p>

<pre><code>
badchans = find(abs(zscore(var(EEG.data ...
    (chansWtEyes,:,:),[],2)))>3);
</code></pre>

<p>
The removed channel information was saved for later interpolation after ICA. The original data, including the removed channel, was also saved for later analysis.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Epoching and Artifact Rejection</h2>

<p>
Continuous EEG data are segmented into epochs based on stimulus markers (<code>s4</code>, <code>s5</code>, <code>s6</code>, <code>s7</code>).
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_epoch( EEG, ...
    { 's4' 's5' 's6' 's7' }, ...
    [-1 2]);
</code></pre>

<p>
Automatic artifact rejection is then applied to remove epochs with excessive amplitudes.
</p>

<pre><code>
[EEG, rmepochs] = pop_autorej( ...
    EEG, ...
    'threshold',150);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Save Preprocessed Dataset</h2>

<p>
Finally, the preprocessed EEG datasets are saved as EEGLAB <code>.set</code> files for ICA analysis.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_saveset( EEG, ...
    'filename', ...
    [data_list(j).name(1:5), ...
    '_chanedit_interp_resemple_filter_ref_epoch.set']);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<p>
At the end of this step:
</p>

<ul>
<li>additional channels have been removed</li>
<li>channel locations have been corrected</li>
<li>data have been filtered and re-referenced</li>
<li>bad channels have been identified</li>
<li>continuous EEG data have been epoched</li>
<li>datasets are ready for ICA decomposition</li>
</ul>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 3. Run ICA and Identify Artifact Components</strong></summary>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to perform Independent Component Analysis (ICA) on the preprocessed EEG data and identify artifact-related components, such as eye movements activity.
</p>

<p>
ICA helps separate neural activity from non-neural artifacts, which improves the quality of ERP analyses.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Initialize EEGLAB Environment</h2>

<p>
The script first initializes the EEGLAB environment and defines the input and output directories.
</p>

<pre><code>
clc;
clear;

eeglab_root = 'D:\setup\matlab\toolbox\eeglab11_0_5_4b';
addpath(genpath(eeglab_root));

root_dir='D:\0EEG\2_before_ICA';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\3_run_ICA';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load Preprocessed EEG Datasets</h2>

<p>
The script loops through all stimulation conditions and loads the preprocessed EEG datasets prepared in Step 2.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEGica = pop_loadset(person_list(i).name);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Run Independent Component Analysis (ICA)</h2>

<p>
ICA is applied to decompose the EEG signals into independent components.
</p>

<p>
This procedure helps separate neural activity from artifacts such as eye blinks, eye movements, muscle activity, and electrical noise.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEGica = pop_runica(EEGica,'extended',1, 'interupt','on');
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Save ICA Decomposition Results</h2>

<p>
The ICA results are saved as a new EEGLAB dataset for later processing.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEGica = pop_saveset( ...
    EEGica, ...
    'filename', ...
    [person_list(i).name(1:5),'_runICA.set']);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Reload Original Continuous EEG Data</h2>

<p>
The original continuous EEG dataset is loaded again so that the ICA weights can later be transferred back to the continuous data.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_loadset( ...
    'filename',OrigFile, ...
    'filepath',Sti_root_dir);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Remove Previously Identified Bad Channels</h2>

<p>
Bad channels identified during preprocessing are removed before transferring ICA weights.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_select(EEG, ...
    'nochannel', badChans );
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Transfer ICA Weights</h2>

<p>
The ICA weights calculated from the epoched data are transferred to the original continuous EEG dataset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_editset( ...
    EEG, ...
    'icachansind', 'EEGica.icachansind', ...
    'icaweights', 'EEGica.icaweights', ...
    'icasphere', 'EEGica.icasphere');
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Automatic IC Classification</h2>

<p>
The ICLabel plugin is used to automatically classify ICA components into categories such as brain activity, eye activity, muscle activity, and noise.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = iclabel(EEG);
</code></pre>

<p>
Eye-related components with a probability above 30% are identified for later removal.
</p>

<pre><code>
eyeRemComp = find(indIC == 3 & v > 0.30);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Generate ICA Topographic Maps</h2>

<p>
Topographic maps of ICA components are generated and saved as PDF files for visual inspection.
</p>

<pre><code>
pop_topoplot(EEG, 0, 1:EEG.nbchan, ...
    person_list(i).name(1:5), ...
    [6 6],0,'electrodes','on');
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Export ICA Component Properties</h2>

<p>
Component properties for each ICA component are exported as PDF files to assist with manual review and artifact identification.
</p>

<pre><code>
pop_prop(EEG, 0, comp);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Save Final ICA Dataset</h2>

<p>
Finally, the dataset containing ICA weights and component classifications is saved for the next analysis step.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_saveset(EEG,'filename', postICAFilename);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<p>
At the end of this step:
</p>

<ul>
<li>ICA decomposition has been completed</li>
<li>artifact-related components have been identified</li>
<li>ICA topographic maps and component reports have been generated</li>
<li>ICA weights have been transferred to the continuous EEG data</li>
<li>datasets are ready for artifact component removal</li>
</ul>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 4. Remove Artifact Components and Interpolate Bad Channels</strong></summary>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to remove artifact-related ICA components and reconstruct previously excluded bad channels.
</p>

<p>
Both automatically identified eye-related components and manually inspected noise components are removed from the EEG data.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Initialize EEGLAB Environment</h2>

<p>
The script first initializes the EEGLAB environment and defines the input and output directories.
</p>

<pre><code>
clc;
clear;

eeglab_root = 'D:\setup\matlab\toolbox\eeglab11_0_5_4b';
addpath(genpath(eeglab_root));

root_dir1='D:\0EEG\2_before_ICA';
root_dir='D:\0EEG\3_run_ICA';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\4_afterICA';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load ICA Datasets</h2>

<p>
The script loops through all stimulation conditions and loads the EEG datasets containing ICA weights and IC classifications.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_loadset(person_list(i).name);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load Automatically Identified Eye Components</h2>

<p>
Eye-related ICA components identified by ICLabel during Step 3 are loaded for removal.
</p>

<pre><code>
eyeCompFile = fullfile( ...
    Sti_root_dir, ...
    [person_list(i).name(1:5), ...
    '_IClabel_eyecomps.txt']);

eyeRemComp = readmatrix(eyeCompFile);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load Manually Inspected Noise Components</h2>

<p>
Additional noise components identified through visual inspection are loaded from an Excel file.
</p>

<pre><code>
file_visually_inspect = fullfile( ...
    root_dir, ...
    'visually_inspect_component_remove.xlsx');

[ndata, text, alldata] = ...
    xlsread(file_visually_inspect);
</code></pre>

<p>
These components may include muscle artifacts, electrical noise, or other non-neural activity that was not fully detected automatically.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Combine Components for Removal</h2>

<p>
The automatically identified eye components and manually identified noise components are combined into one list of components to remove.
</p>

<pre><code>
components_Removed = ...
    [eyeRemComp' noise_components];
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Remove Artifact Components</h2>

<p>
The selected ICA components are removed from the EEG data using <code>pop_subcomp()</code>.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_subcomp( ...
    EEG, ...
    components_Removed, ...
    0);
</code></pre>

<p>
This step reduces artifacts such as eye blinks, eye movements, muscle activity, and electrical noise while preserving neural signals.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Interpolate Previously Removed Bad Channels</h2>

<p>
If bad channels were removed during preprocessing, they are reconstructed using spherical interpolation.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_interp( ...
    EEG, ...
    EEGorigChans.chanlocs, ...
    'spherical');
</code></pre>

<p>
After interpolation, the standard channel locations are restored.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_chanedit( ...
    EEG, ...
    'lookup', ...
    'standard-10-5-cap385.elp');
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Save Final EEG Dataset</h2>

<p>
Finally, the cleaned EEG dataset is saved for later ERP analysis.
</p>

<pre><code>
file_afterICA = fullfile( ...
    Sti_out_dir, ...
    [person_list(i).name(1:5), ...
    '_afterICA.set']);

EEG = pop_saveset( ...
    EEG, ...
    'filename', ...
    file_afterICA);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<p>
At the end of this step:
</p>

<ul>
<li>artifact-related ICA components have been removed</li>
<li>eye and noise artifacts have been reduced</li>
<li>previously excluded bad channels have been interpolated</li>
<li>cleaned EEG datasets have been generated</li>
<li>the datasets are ready for ERP extraction and statistical analysis</li>
</ul>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 5. Epoch EEG Data and Reject Artifacts</strong></summary>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to segment the continuous EEG recordings into event-related epochs and remove remaining artifacts before ERP averaging.
</p>

<p>
The EEG data are epoched based on stimulus event markers, baseline-corrected, and cleaned using automatic artifact rejection criteria.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Initialize EEGLAB Environment</h2>

<p>
The script first initializes the EEGLAB environment and defines the input and output directories.
</p>

<pre><code>
clc;
clear;

eeglab_root = 'D:\setup\matlab\toolbox\eeglab11_0_5_4b';
addpath(genpath(eeglab_root));

root_dir='D:\0EEG\4_afterICA';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\5_epoch';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load Cleaned EEG Datasets</h2>

<p>
The script loops through all stimulation conditions and loads the cleaned EEG datasets generated after ICA artifact removal.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_loadset(data_list(j).name);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Define Epoch and Baseline Parameters</h2>

<p>
Epoching and artifact rejection parameters are defined before processing.
</p>

<pre><code>
Upper_Limit = 80;
Lower_Limit = -80;

Baseline_cue = [-100 0];

Epoch_Start2_cue = -0.101;
Epoch_End3_cue = 0.601;
</code></pre>

<p>
The EEG epochs are extracted from approximately -100 ms to 600 ms relative to stimulus onset.
</p>

<p>
Baseline correction is applied using the pre-stimulus interval from -100 ms to 0 ms.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Define Event Markers</h2>

<p>
The EEG data are segmented based on four event markers:
</p>

<ul>
<li><code>s4</code></li>
<li><code>s5</code></li>
<li><code>s6</code></li>
<li><code>s7</code></li>
</ul>

<pre><code>
mark = { 's4' 's5' 's6' 's7'};
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Epoch EEG Data</h2>

<p>
For each event marker, continuous EEG data are segmented into time-locked epochs.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_epoch( ...
    EEG, ...
    { mark{m} }, ...
    [Epoch_Start2_cue Epoch_End3_cue]);
</code></pre>

<p>
Standard 64-channel electrode locations are also reloaded before epoching.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG=pop_chanedit(EEG, ...
    'lookup', ...
    'standard-10-5-cap385.elp');
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Baseline Correction</h2>

<p>
Baseline correction is applied to remove slow voltage shifts before stimulus onset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_rmbase(EEG, Baseline_cue);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Automatic Artifact Rejection</h2>

<p>
Epochs containing excessive amplitudes are automatically rejected using a voltage threshold of ±80 μV.
</p>

<pre><code>
[EEG Indexes] = pop_eegthresh( ...
    EEG, ...
    1, ...
    [1:A], ...
    Lower_Limit, ...
    Upper_Limit, ...
    BaselineStart2_cue, ...
    Epoch_End2_cue, ...
    2, ...
    0);
</code></pre>

<p>
The rejected epochs are then removed from the dataset.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_rejepoch(EEG, Indexes, 0);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Save Epoched EEG Datasets</h2>

<p>
The cleaned and epoched EEG datasets are saved separately for each event condition.
</p>

<pre><code>
EEG = pop_saveset( ...
    EEG, ...
    'filename', ...
    EEG.setname, ...
    'filepath', ...
    file_epoch);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<p>
At the end of this step:
</p>

<ul>
<li>continuous EEG data have been segmented into epochs</li>
<li>baseline correction has been applied</li>
<li>artifact-contaminated epochs have been removed</li>
<li>condition-specific EEG datasets have been generated</li>
<li>the datasets are ready for ERP averaging and N2 analysis</li>
</ul>

<details>
<summary><strong>Step 6. Extract N2 Component and Plot ERP Waveforms</strong></summary>

<p>
The purpose of this step is to calculate the N2 ERP component and generate ERP waveforms for each experimental condition.
</p>

<p>
After preprocessing and artifact removal, the epoched EEG datasets are averaged across trials and participants to obtain condition-specific ERP responses.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Define Input and Output Directories</h2>

<p>
The script first defines the directories containing the epoched EEG datasets and the output directory for ERP results.
</p>

<pre><code>
root_dir='D:\0EEG\5_epoch';
out_dir='D:\0EEG\6_average';
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Define Experimental Conditions</h2>

<p>
The four experimental conditions are defined according to language and switching sequence:
</p>

<ul>
<li>L1 repeat</li>
<li>L1 switch</li>
<li>L2 repeat</li>
<li>L2 switch</li>
</ul>

<pre><code>
condition_names = { ...
    'L1 repeat', ...
    'L1 switch', ...
    'L2 repeat', ...
    'L2 switch'};
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Load and Average EEG Epochs</h2>

<p>
For each stimulation condition and event marker, all participant EEG datasets are loaded and averaged across trials.
</p>

<pre><code>
data(:,:,i)=squeeze(mean(EEG.data,3));
</code></pre>

<p>
This step converts trial-level EEG data into participant-level ERP averages.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Combine All Experimental Conditions</h2>

<p>
The averaged ERP data from all event conditions are combined into one matrix for later analysis.
</p>

<pre><code>
total_data(:,:,:,k)=data;
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Define the N2 Time Window</h2>

<p>
The N2 component is calculated within the 200–350 ms time window after stimulus onset.
</p>

<pre><code>
windowstar=200;
windoeend=350;
</code></pre>

<p>
The analysis focuses on frontal and fronto-central electrodes commonly associated with the N2 component.
</p>

<pre><code>
N2_onlychannel = DATA( ...
    [1 21 2 22 3 24 5 25 ...
     6 26 8 28 9 29 10], ...
    :,:,:,:);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Calculate Mean N2 Amplitudes</h2>

<p>
The ERP amplitudes are averaged across both time points and selected electrodes to obtain the mean N2 amplitude for each participant and condition.
</p>

<pre><code>
N2_average = squeeze( ...
    mean( ...
    squeeze(mean(N2_onlychannel,2)), ...
    1));
</code></pre>

<p>
The results are exported as an Excel file for later statistical analysis.
</p>

<pre><code>
writecell(excel_cell, ...
    [stimulation{a},'_N2_average.xlsx']);
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Generate ERP Waveforms</h2>

<p>
Grand-average ERP waveforms are calculated and plotted across conditions.
</p>

<pre><code>
avgtotle_data_N2 = squeeze( ...
    mean( ...
    mean(total_data(channel_N2,:,:,:),3),1));
</code></pre>

<p>
The ERP plots display voltage changes over time, with negative amplitudes plotted upward following ERP conventions.
</p>

<pre><code>
set(gca,'YDir','reverse')
</code></pre>

<hr>

<h2>Plot Condition-Specific ERP Curves</h2>

<p>
Separate ERP curves are plotted for each experimental condition using different colors.
</p>

<pre><code>
plot(EEG.times,avgtotle_data_N2(:,1))
